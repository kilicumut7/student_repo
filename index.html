Newton's Laws of Motion

First Law: Law of Inertia

An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force.

Second Law: F = ma

The acceleration of an object is proportional to the net force applied and inversely proportional to its mass.

Formula: F = m × a

Third Law: Action and Reaction

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Projectile Motion

Objects thrown into the air follow a curved path due to gravity and initial velocity.

  • Horizontal motion with constant velocity
  • Vertical motion with constant acceleration (gravity)
  • Key parameters:
    • Range
    • Maximum height
    • Time of flight

Energy Conservation

The total energy in an isolated system remains constant.

  • Kinetic Energy (KE): KE = ½mv²
  • Potential Energy (PE): PE = mgh
  • Mechanical Energy = KE + PE
